About Valentine

Special Operative Juniper James Valentine landed on Earth in 1947 with absolutely no knowledge of his past. However, he regards this as more of a clean slate than a tragic loss. Unfortunately, premonitions and hallucinations taunt him into thinking his aura is innately corrupt, despite the consolation of his fellow people at the Supernatural Assembly (better known as the Assocation) that he is a vessel of pureblooded light magic!
Fast forward eighty years, James has experienced a lot of what Earth has to offer, while still being a quiet member of society. In fact, he is so reserved that he has avoided relationships and work commitments. An honorable and intelligent man who does not mind not knowing who he was in the past, James lives a self-satisfied life, even taking pride in his low-profile identity. But a sudden accident paves the way for darkness to return to his soul, threatening to undo the mien of normalcy that James has been keeping up for so long. This new, unwelcome magic within him proves to be so conniving and malevolent that James quickly realizes he cannot fight it alone.
Though reluctant, James must accept the aid of a group of detached students of the Association. However, the more hands that are outstretched toward him, the more deception he faces. In a world where one doesn't know who to trust, will James ever find his way out of his internal dilemma? Filled with altercations between longtime acquaintances, magical battles, and a constantly morphing definition of the difference between right and wrong, there is never a dull moment in the life of S.O. Valentine.

About the Author

Alias Callisto Lyfaeren
Yup! Just me! Artist, scriptor, editor (risky! I'm aware), etc. Like that Leslie Knope credits scene. Not that I intended to work alone, (I'm not Batman, and even Batman has Alfred.) but that's sort of how it went. I do credit the website template to Start Bootstrap's Grayscale.
I'm years old. Planning to major in biology or physiology, I also like watermelons and shirts with collars on them. My great fondness for uniforms arose from that idyll called middle school, where they brainwashed me into believing that uniform clothing was the solution to life. Defiant I was, and from an elementary school with sartorial freedom, yet they hushed me.
"Imagine," they said. "Imagine if you could pulverize all the trouble of thinking. You see, the only solution is not to think! If you could be like everyone else, if we could eliminate the characteristic teenage struggle of contrast. No more competition, no more conflict, no need to fret about standing out if everyone looks exactly the same! Live your life, dictated by the educational institution. Furthermore, why subject yourself to the arduous task of decision-making? Why set your own priorities when they will only have to be corrected later in life? Let us decide for you; let us set your priorities."
And that's basically where I got the idea for Valentine.
