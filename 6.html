UPDATES ON MONDAYS

Page 6

posted 23 Dec. 2020
"There was something off about me. The way I was drawn? The fact that I'm being drawn by a fourteen-year-old?" Anyhoo, I did try, and upon reflection, I imagine I did at least a mediocre job. Unlike the storytelling. Ever heard of that pattern in video games and whatnot where your avatar/main character gets dropped into a totally weird and quirky world, and then the "God/dess" character appears and goes all, "Do you want to know why you have been summoned to this world? Well, complete these fifty quests and as a reward I'll let you have a long and purposeless chat with me!" And then POOF, they disappear into thin air. This is the sort of trope that the "sprightly spirit" falls into. What's with this sassy... lost child?
On the art side of things, I'm kind of fond of artificial backgrounds with whimsical circles, as seen on the last panel. For one thing, I don't have to do all that work drawing background stuff that takes half an hour to get straight that literally no one even looks at except for me. Another thing is that adding too much background furniture just kind of explodes in my face, since I would have to make the characters higher in contrast than the background. In shorter terms, my art style is basically me going "YEET-"
